The charter
This is not a job hunt. It is a fifteen-year campaign.
Every application logged here is a move in that campaign. Every cold outreach is a reconnaissance contact. Every milestone is confirmed ground taken. The pipeline does not measure activity — it measures progress against a defined endpoint. The conventional candidate competes for the role they are qualified for today. The operator competes for the role they will be qualified for in three years, by being demonstrably better than the credentialed candidates who never bothered to prove anything.
The endpoint — non-negotiable
By age 25
$85K
Bridge role secured. CFA Level 1. Trading record live.
By age 28
$200K+
S&T or capital seat. New York. Track record verified.
By age 30
$400K+
Comp accelerating. Capital base compounding. Autonomous.
By age 35
$1M+
Senior seat or own capital. Complete sovereignty.
The person who succeeds on this path is comfortable being underestimated for years without needing external validation to stay motivated. They think in systems, not events. They hold a five-year vision clearly while executing with urgency today. They extract value from every environment, especially the boring ones. They don't need the path to feel fair — they accept that it isn't, and compete anyway.
— Who you have to be
Charter
What I must be
  • Patient enough for compound positioning, urgent enough never to drift
  • Honest about gaps without becoming defeatist about them
  • Willing to take asymmetric bets and accept short-term inferiority of position
  • Capable of extracting value from every environment, including the boring ones
  • Comfortable in rooms where my credentials don't yet reflect my thinking
  • Loyal to the long-term outcome over the short-term emotion
What I refuse to accept as truth
  • "Your GPA is too low for that"
  • "You should be happy with what's available"
  • "The bridge job is fine, why are you still pushing"
  • "People from your background don't end up there"
  • "The conventional path is the safe path"
  • "Stop comparing yourself to the kids from target schools"
What I am actually optimizing
  • Not for the next role — for the role four steps ahead
  • Not for income at 24 — for compounding through 35
  • Not for credentials I can show — for the proof of work that makes credentials irrelevant
  • Not for the version of me my résumé describes — for the version of me the work demands
  • Not for comfort — for the elimination of the gap between who I am and how I operate
The disciplines that keep this real
  • Twenty tailored applications per month, every month, without exception
  • Fifteen cold outreaches per month — every contact logged, followed up exactly once after five business days
  • Portfolio projects shipped on schedule — the work is the credential
  • Trading track record documented daily — the record is the resume the GPA can't write
  • CFA progression on the published cadence — Level 1 by 25, Level 2 by 27, Level 3 by 29
  • Geographic readiness at all times — New York is not optional, it is the gate
The traditional candidate competes for jobs they are qualified for. I compete for jobs I am not yet qualified for, by being demonstrably better than the people who are. The market for credentialed candidates is brutal because supply meets demand. The market for proven operators is wide open because almost nobody actually proves anything. I'm the operator who proves it.
— The game I'm playing

Five phases. Twelve years. Each phase has a job, a job inside the job, a target outcome, and a way it can fail. Comfort is the only thing that kills it. The bridge job is not the destination. The first New York seat is not the destination. The destination is sovereignty.

Phase 01 — Foundation
23–24
2026 – 2027
Graduate. Set the table. Start the clock.
Primary objective
Pass FIRE 311 with a C or better. Finish at 3.0 cumulative. Secure the first finance-adjacent role in the DC/MD market before graduation. Begin trading real capital — small, disciplined, fully documented.
Location & comp
DC / Maryland market — softer GPA filters, real finance operations, accessible cold outreach.
$60K – $85K
Roles in play
Financial analyst at a GSE (Freddie Mac, Fannie Mae). Investment analyst at a DC boutique asset manager. Credit or financial analyst at a regional bank. Finance rotational at a defense contractor.
Job inside the job
Learn the language of institutional finance. Build the trading record in parallel. Save aggressively — every dollar not spent compounds the operation. Never become comfortable.
How this phase fails
FIRE 311 below a C — collapses the academic sequence. Drift into a non-analytical role and call it good enough. Treat the bridge job as the destination. Skip the trading record because life gets busy.
Phase 02 — Credential stack
24–26
2027 – 2029
CFA Level 1. Real money documented. The resume rewrites itself.
Primary objective
CFA Level 1 passed within 12 months of graduating. Eighteen months of documented, consistent trading. CFA Level 2 in motion. Capital base building.
Location & comp
Still DC/MD, but with NY recruiting active in the background. Position is becoming portable.
$80K – $120K
The lateral move
By the end of year 1, reposition into a role with market exposure or P&L visibility. Junior trader support, sell-side research associate, investment analyst at a midsize fund. The role that gets you on the desk.
Job inside the job
Become the most prepared person in every interview. Translate the trading record into the language of the firm you're targeting. Make sure the next role pays $100K+ and has direct path to market exposure.
How this phase fails
CFA delayed past 25 — momentum lost. Trading record patchy or undocumented — the proof evaporates. Stay in the bridge role past 24 months because the title gets bigger but the role doesn't.
Phase 03 — The crossing
26–28
2029 – 2031
New York. The first real seat.
Primary objective
Relocate to New York. Land S&T at a regional or mid-tier firm, or a junior trader role at a prop desk, or senior investment analyst at an asset manager with meaningful market exposure. CFA Level 2 done. Level 3 in motion.
Location & comp
New York. The DC ceiling is real, and you've hit it. The track record is now 3+ years documented.
$150K – $250K
Target firms
Stifel, Baird, Raymond James, Piper Sandler S&T. Susquehanna, DRW, IMC junior trader. Mid-tier asset managers — Two Sigma quant ops, Millennium, Citadel non-investment seats. Or independent prop with verified track.
Job inside the job
Build the desk relationships that compound for the next decade. Be the analyst who knows their book better than anyone. Make P&L. The number on screen is the only language that matters from here on.
How this phase fails
Hesitate on the move because DC feels safer. Take a New York role with no market exposure because it pays more on paper. Get a seat and play it safe — there are no points for being in the building if you never speak.
Phase 04 — Acceleration
28–32
2031 – 2035
Performance compounds. The credentials stop mattering.
Primary objective
Hit the first $300K+ comp year. Move from mid-tier to upper-mid or top-tier firm — the P&L makes the recruiter call you. CFA Level 3. Begin building external capital independently.
Location & comp
New York or top trading hub (Chicago for some prop, Stamford for some funds). Comp now performance-tied — base is half the story.
$300K – $700K
The leverage move
By 30, you have leverage. Either move to a top firm where your P&L gets a larger share, or begin raising outside capital to run independently. Don't stay in a seat where the upside is capped by someone else's payout structure.
Job inside the job
Become known for one specific edge or instrument. Be the person in the room people quote. Build the public reputation that makes the next move chosen for you, not by you.
How this phase fails
Stay in the same seat past your contribution. Get comfortable with $300K and stop pushing. Burn out and revert. Let firm politics determine seat allocation when your number says otherwise.
Phase 05 — Sovereignty
32–40+
2035+
Senior seat, own book, or own firm. The endpoint of the campaign.
Primary objective
$1M+ comp year. Senior trader, portfolio manager, or principal of own capital. Capital base sufficient to operate without firm dependency. Complete integration of how I think and how I get paid.
Location & comp
Wherever is optimal — geography becomes optionality.
$1M+
The optionality
Senior PM at top fund. Founding partner or principal at a new venture. Independent operator running own book. Or transitioning capital into adjacent edge — angel investment, founder-equity stakes. Every option is on the table.
The endpoint of the endpoint
The gap between who I am and how I operate is closed. The view is a consequence of the work, not a goal of the work. The campaign is over because there is no remaining underdog position to overcome.

These are the checkpoints. Every milestone has a target age. Every milestone confirms the campaign is on schedule. Check them off when they're real — not when they feel close.
