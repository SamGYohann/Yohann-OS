Five artifacts. Each one is concrete — something you can send, show, or discuss in an interview. The discipline of shipping them on schedule is itself the signal. Check tasks as you complete them.

Twelve weeks. Each week has a focus. Tap to expand. The schedule is a default, not a cage — but if you're going to break it, break it for more work, not less.

Four domains, ranked by priority. Probability is the foundation everything else sits on. The target levels are deliberately bounded — you are not getting a PhD this summer, you are reaching functional fluency in the things that actually matter on a desk.

Nothing generic. Each resource maps to a specific skill domain and a specific deliverable. Most are free. The constraint is never access — it's consistency.

Two hours minimum, every weekday, outside any job or internship work. The rhythm is the engine. Skill is the residue of consistent reps, not occasional intensity.

The non-negotiables
  • No Python session that only reads documentation — every session produces output
  • No skipped trade log — every position has a thesis before entry and an assessment after exit
  • No "I'll double up tomorrow" — the rhythm compounds only if it's daily
  • No deliverable started in the final week of its window — front-load, always

Real money. Small size. Every trade logged with the probabilistic reasoning behind it — not "the chart looked good."
